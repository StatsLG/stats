Standard Deviation of a Discrete Probability Distribution

Variation of Discrete Probability Distributions

As always, in order to calculate standard deviation, we must start by calculating the variance. The population variance for a discrete probability distribution is:

\[ \sigma^2 = \sum (x - \mu)^2 P(x) = \sum x^2 P(x) - \mu^2 \]

The population standard deviation is the square root of the variance:

\[ \sigma = \sqrt{\sigma^2} \]

\( \sigma \) is a lowercase Greek letter said as ‘sigma’ (Can be any nonnegative real number)

Example 1

Roll a die and \( x \) is the number rolled. Find the standard deviation of \( x \).

Let's find the standard deviation of \( x \):

For a fair six-sided die, possible outcomes are 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, each with \( P(x) = \frac{1}{6} \). First, we need the mean (\( \mu \)) from the expected value:

\[ \mu = (1 \cdot \frac{1}{6}) + (2 \cdot \frac{1}{6}) + (3 \cdot \frac{1}{6}) + (4 \cdot \frac{1}{6}) + (5 \cdot \frac{1}{6}) + (6 \cdot \frac{1}{6}) = \frac{21}{6} = 3.5 \]

Now, calculate variance (\( \sigma^2 \)) using \( \sigma^2 = \sum x^2 P(x) - \mu^2 \):

\[ \sum x^2 P(x) = (1^2 \cdot \frac{1}{6}) + (2^2 \cdot \frac{1}{6}) + (3^2 \cdot \frac{1}{6}) + (4^2 \cdot \frac{1}{6}) + (5^2 \cdot \frac{1}{6}) + (6^2 \cdot \frac{1}{6}) \]

\[ = (\frac{1}{6}) + (\frac{4}{6}) + (\frac{9}{6}) + (\frac{16}{6}) + (\frac{25}{6}) + (\frac{36}{6}) = \frac{1 + 4 + 9 + 16 + 25 + 36}{6} = \frac{91}{6} \approx 15.1667 \]

\[ \mu^2 = (3.5)^2 = 12.25 \]

\[ \sigma^2 = \frac{91}{6} - 12.25 = 15.1667 - 12.25 = 2.9167 \]

Standard deviation (\( \sigma \)):

\[ \sigma = \sqrt{\sigma^2} = \sqrt{2.9167} \approx 1.708 \]

So, the standard deviation of \( x \) is approximately \( \sigma \approx 1.708 \).

Example 2

A game involves spinning a spinner divided into three sections:

  • Section A (50% of the area): Win 2 points
  • Section B (30% of the area): Win 5 points
  • Section C (20% of the area): Lose 1 point (i.e., -1 point)

Let \( x \) be the number of points won or lost in one spin. Find the standard deviation of \( x \).

Let's find the standard deviation of \( x \):

The probability distribution is:

\( x \)\( P(x) \)
20.5
50.3
-10.2

First, calculate the mean (\( \mu \)):

\[ \mu = (2 \cdot 0.5) + (5 \cdot 0.3) + (-1 \cdot 0.2) = 1 + 1.5 - 0.2 = 2.3 \]

Now, calculate variance (\( \sigma^2 \)) using \( \sigma^2 = \sum x^2 P(x) - \mu^2 \):

\[ \sum x^2 P(x) = (2^2 \cdot 0.5) + (5^2 \cdot 0.3) + ((-1)^2 \cdot 0.2) \]

\[ = (4 \cdot 0.5) + (25 \cdot 0.3) + (1 \cdot 0.2) = 2 + 7.5 + 0.2 = 9.7 \]

\[ \mu^2 = (2.3)^2 = 5.29 \]

\[ \sigma^2 = 9.7 - 5.29 = 4.41 \]

Standard deviation (\( \sigma \)):

\[ \sigma = \sqrt{\sigma^2} = \sqrt{4.41} = 2.1 \]

So, the standard deviation of \( x \) is \( \sigma = 2.1 \) points.
