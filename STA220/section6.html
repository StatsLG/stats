Continuous RVs & Density Functions

A continuous random variable takes values on an interval. We model its distribution with a smooth curve \(f(x)\) called a density function. The probability of falling in an interval is the area under \(f(x)\) over that interval.

Properties of a Density Function

  • \(f(x)\) is a function (passes the vertical line test).
  • \(f(x)\ge 0\) for all \(x\) (probabilities are never negative).
  • Total area under \(f(x)\) is \(1\) (probability over the whole sample space is 1).

Interval Probability vs. Points

For continuous variables we assign probability to intervals, not points:

\(P(a\le x\le b)\) is the area under \(f(x)\) from \(a\) to \(b\).

At a single point, \(P(x=a)=0\). A “point” has zero width, so its area is zero. Also, with infinitely many possible outcomes, any specific point has probability \(0\).

Therefore, \(P(a\le x\le b)=P(a<x<b)\); inclusion of endpoints doesn’t change the value.

Example — \(f(x)=2x\) on \(0\le x\le 1\)

Draw & reason

The graph is a straight line from \((0,0)\) to \((1,2)\), forming a right triangle with the \(x\)-axis.

(a) Total area

\(\displaystyle \text{Area}=\tfrac12\cdot \text{base}\cdot \text{height}=\tfrac12\cdot1\cdot2=1\).

(b) \(P(0\le x\le 0.5)\)

\(\displaystyle \tfrac12\cdot 0.5\cdot 1 = 0.25\).

(c) \(P(0.5\le x\le 1)\)

Total area \(1\) minus area from \(0\) to \(0.5\): \(1-0.25=0.75\).

(d) \(P(0.25\le x\le 0.75)\)

Area \([0,0.75]\): \(\tfrac12\cdot0.75\cdot1.5=0.5625\); area \([0,0.25]\): \(\tfrac12\cdot0.25\cdot0.5=0.0625\); difference \(=0.50\).

Mean & Standard Deviation for Continuous RVs (note)

The definitions mirror the discrete case but use calculus (integrals). In this course we’ll focus on specific continuous families (like the uniform) where results are known.

The Uniform Distribution

Flat (constant) density on an interval \([a,b]\):

\[ f(x) = \frac{1}{\,b-a\,}, \qquad a\le x \le b \]

Important

  • Always restrict the domain to \([a,b]\); outside that interval the density is \(0\).
  • Total area \(=\) height \(\times\) width \(= \tfrac{1}{b-a}\cdot (b-a)=1\).

Example — Uniform with \(a=2,\ b=4\)

Density

\(f(x)=\tfrac{1}{4-2}=\tfrac12\) for \(2\le x\le4\).

Total area

Rectangle area \(=\ell\cdot w=(2)\cdot(\tfrac12)=1\).

\(P(2\le x\le 3)\)

Width \(1\) \(\times\) height \(\tfrac12 = 0.5\).

Mean

Symmetric about \(x=3\Rightarrow \mu=3\).

Uniform Shortcuts

Interval probability

\(\displaystyle P(c\le x\le d)=\frac{d-c}{\,b-a\,}\quad (a\le c\le d\le b)\)

Mean

\(\displaystyle \mu=\frac{a+b}{2}\)

Variance / SD

\(\displaystyle \sigma^2=\frac{(b-a)^2}{12},\quad \sigma=\sqrt{\sigma^2}\)

Median / Mode / Range

Median \(=\mu\) (symmetry), Mode = D.N.E. (flat), Range \(=b-a\).

Example — Travel Time \(200\)–\(240\) minutes (Uniform)

Setup

\(a=200,\ b=240\Rightarrow f(x)=\tfrac{1}{40}\) for \(200\le x\le240\).

\(P(200\le x<225)\)

\(\displaystyle \frac{225-200}{240-200}=\frac{25}{40}=\frac58=0.625\).

Mean & Median

\(\mu=\frac{200+240}{2}=220\); median \(=220\).

Mode & Range

Mode = D.N.E.; Range \(=240-200=40\).

Variance & SD

\(\sigma^2=\frac{40^2}{12}=133.333\);\; \(\sigma\approx 11.547\).
