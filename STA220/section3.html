Measures of Dispersion

Measures of spread complement measures of center. Two data sets can share mean/median/mode yet differ in variability.

Sample Range

Range = High − Low; easy but highly sensitive to extremes.

Sample Variance and Standard Deviation

Variance measures the average squared distance from the mean. Two equivalent formulas:

  • Conceptual: s^2 = \frac{\sum (x - \bar{x})^2}{n-1}
  • Computational: s^2 = \frac{n\sum x^2 - (\sum x)^2}{n(n-1)}

Key facts: involves all data points; expressed in squared units; never negative; larger values ⇒ more variability. Also, \sum (x-\bar{x}) = 0.
