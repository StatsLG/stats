Sampling Distribution of \(\hat{p}\)

Estimating the Population Proportion

The sample proportion \(\hat{p}\) estimates the population proportion \(p\):

  • Population Proportion: \( p = \frac{\text{\# in population with characteristic}}{\text{\# in population}} \)
  • Sample Proportion: \( \hat{p} = \frac{\text{\# in sample with characteristic}}{n} \)

Key Properties

  • \(\mu_{\hat{p}} = p\)
    The average value of \(\hat{p}\) across all possible samples equals \(p\).
  • \(\sigma_{\hat{p}} = \sqrt{\frac{p(1 - p)}{n}}\)
    The standard error of \(\hat{p}\) is \(\sqrt{\frac{p(1 - p)}{n}}\).

Normal Approximation

For large \(n\): \[ Z = \frac{\hat{p} - p}{\sqrt{\frac{p(1 - p)}{n}}} \] is approximately standard normal.

Examples

Example 1: Stats Class Pass Rates

A university claims that 75% of students pass their introductory statistics course. A random sample of 80 students is taken.

  1. Find \(\mu_{\hat{p}}\).
  2. Find \(\sigma_{\hat{p}}\).

1. \(\mu_{\hat{p}} = p = 0.75\)

2. \(\sigma_{\hat{p}} = \sqrt{\frac{p(1 - p)}{n}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.75 \cdot (1 - 0.75)}{80}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.75 \cdot 0.25}{80}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.1875}{80}} = \sqrt{0.00234375} \approx 0.0484\)

Example 2: Equine Vaccination Rates

In a survey of 200 horse owners, 160 report that their horses are vaccinated against equine influenza, where \(p\) is the proportion of all horses vaccinated in the population.

  1. Calculate \(\hat{p}\).
  2. Calculate the margin of error for a 95% confidence interval.
  3. Calculate an estimate for the standard deviation of the sampling distribution.
  4. How does the margin of error relate to the standard deviation?

1. \(\hat{p} = \frac{160}{200} = 0.8\)

2. Margin of error (95% CI): \( 1.96 \cdot \sigma_{\hat{p}} = 1.96 \cdot \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1 - \hat{p})}{n}} = 1.96 \cdot \sqrt{\frac{0.8 \cdot 0.2}{200}} = 1.96 \cdot \sqrt{\frac{0.16}{200}} = 1.96 \cdot \sqrt{0.0008} \approx 1.96 \cdot 0.02828 \approx 0.0554 \)

3. Estimated \(\sigma_{\hat{p}} = \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1 - \hat{p})}{n}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.8 \cdot 0.2}{200}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.16}{200}} = \sqrt{0.0008} \approx 0.02828\)

4. The margin of error is \( 1.96 \cdot \sigma_{\hat{p}} \approx 0.0554 \), which is approximately 2 standard deviations (since \( 2 \cdot 0.02828 = 0.05656 \)), aligning with the empirical ruleâ€™s 95% coverage approximation.
