Hurricane Atmospheric Pressure and Windspeed Data

Atmospheric pressure is the force exerted by the weight of air in the atmosphere. It plays a crucial role in weather patterns, including the formation and strength of hurricanes. Low atmospheric pressure systems can lead to the development of strong winds and storm systems, including hurricanes. As pressure drops, wind speeds typically increase, fueling storm intensity.
