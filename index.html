Hurricane Atmospheric Pressure and Windspeed Data

Atmospheric pressure is the force exerted by the weight of air in the atmosphere. It plays a crucial role in weather patterns, including the formation and strength of hurricanes. Low atmospheric pressure systems can lead to the development of strong winds and storm systems, including hurricanes. As pressure drops, wind speeds typically increase, fueling storm intensity.

  4. Predict the windspeed of a hurricane with an atmospheric pressure of 950 millibars.

Results

1. Correlation Coefficient: Approximately -0.535, indicating a moderate negative correlation. As atmospheric pressure decreases, windspeed tends to increase.

2. Best-Fit Line Equation: \( y = -0.4286x + 486.20 \), where \( y \) is windspeed (MPH) and \( x \) is pressure (millibars).

3. Slope Interpretation: The slope of -0.4286 means that for every 1 millibar decrease in pressure, windspeed increases by about 0.4286 MPH. This shows how lower pressure drives stronger hurricane winds.

4. Predicted Windspeed at 950 mb: Using the equation, the windspeed is approximately 79 MPH.
