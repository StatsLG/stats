Valid Discrete Probability Distributions

Intro to Probability Distributions

First, it’s important to distinguish between discrete and continuous random variables. While the concept of a probability distribution applies to both, the approach differs. Here, we focus on discrete random variables.

Discrete Random Variable: Values are distinct points on the number line; finite or countable (e.g., counting outcomes).

Continuous Random Variable: Values form an interval on the number line; infinite (e.g., measuring outcomes).

A discrete random variable has a finite or countable number of outcomes, each assigned a probability.

Probability Distribution: The set of \( P(x) \) values for all possible \( x \).

A valid discrete probability distribution must satisfy:

  1. \( x \) is discrete.
  2. \( 0 \leq P(x) \leq 1 \) for all \( x \).
  3. \( \sum P(x) = 1 \).

Example 1: Coin Flip

Flip a coin twice, where \( x \) = number of tails. Identify the probability distribution for \( x \).

Possible outcomes: HH, HT, TH, TT

\( x \) (number of tails) can be 0, 1, or 2:

  • \( x = 0 \) (HH): \( P(x=0) = \frac{1}{4} = 0.25 \)
  • \( x = 1 \) (HT, TH): \( P(x=1) = \frac{2}{4} = 0.5 \)
  • \( x = 2 \) (TT): \( P(x=2) = \frac{1}{4} = 0.25 \)

Verification: \( \sum P(x) = 0.25 + 0.5 + 0.25 = 1 \), all \( P(x) \) between 0 and 1. Valid distribution.

Example 2: Card Draw

Draw one card from a 52-card deck, where \( x = 1 \) if it’s a face card (Jack, Queen, King), \( x = 0 \) if not. Identify the probability distribution for \( x \).

Face cards: 3 per suit × 4 suits = 12. Non-face cards: 52 - 12 = 40.

  • \( x = 0 \) (non-face): \( P(x=0) = \frac{40}{52} = \frac{10}{13} \approx 0.7692 \)
  • \( x = 1 \) (face): \( P(x=1) = \frac{12}{52} = \frac{3}{13} \approx 0.2308 \)

Example 3: Powerball Lottery

In a simplified Powerball, a ticket costs $2, and the jackpot is $400M with \( P(\text{win}) = \frac{1}{292,201,338} \). Define \( X \) as net gain. Find the probability distribution, expected value, and standard deviation.

\( X \) = net gain:

  • Win: \( X = 400,000,000 - 2 = 399,999,998 \), \( P(X = 399,999,998) = \frac{1}{292,201,338} \approx 0.00000000342 \)
  • Lose: \( X = -2 \), \( P(X = -2) = \frac{292,201,337}{292,201,338} \approx 0.99999999658 \)
\( X \)\( P(X) \)
399,999,998\( \frac{1}{292,201,338} \approx 0.00000000342 \)
-2\( \frac{292,201,337}{292,201,338} \approx 0.99999999658 \)

Verification: \( \sum P(X) = 1 \), all \( P(X) \) between 0 and 1. Valid distribution.

Expected Value \( E(X) \): \( E(X) = (399,999,998) \cdot \frac{1}{292,201,338} + (-2) \cdot \frac{292,201,337}{292,201,338} \approx 1.3688 - 2.0000 \approx -0.6312 \) ($-0.63 loss per ticket).

Variance \( \text{Var}(X) \): \( E(X^2) = (399,999,998)^2 \cdot \frac{1}{292,201,338} + 4 \cdot \frac{292,201,337}{292,201,338} \approx 547,943,801 \), \( [E(X)]^2 \approx 0.3984 \), \( \text{Var}(X) \approx 547,943,800 \).

Standard Deviation \( \sigma(X) \): \( \sigma(X) = \sqrt{547,943,800} \approx 23,407 \).

Note: Ignores smaller prizes, taxes, and multiple winners for simplicity.

Example 4: Roulette Wheel Bet

In American roulette, a wheel has 38 slots: numbers 1-36, 0, and 00. You bet $1 on a single number (e.g., 17). If your number hits, you win $35 plus your $1 back; otherwise, you lose the $1. Define \( X \) as the net gain from this bet. Identify the probability distribution for \( X \), then calculate its expected value and standard deviation.

\( X \) = net gain. Total slots = 38. Outcomes:

  • Win (number hits): \( X = 35 + 1 - 1 = 35 \) (win $35, keep $1, minus bet), 1 way, \( P(X=35) = \frac{1}{38} \approx 0.0263 \)
  • Lose (number misses): \( X = 0 - 1 = -1 \) (lose $1), 37 ways, \( P(X=-1) = \frac{37}{38} \approx 0.9737 \)
\( X \)\( P(X) \)
35\( \frac{1}{38} \approx 0.0263 \)
-1\( \frac{37}{38} \approx 0.9737 \)

Verification: \( \sum P(X) = \frac{1}{38} + \frac{37}{38} = 1 \), all \( P(X) \) between 0 and 1. Valid distribution.

Expected Value \( E(X) \): \( E(X) = 35 \cdot \frac{1}{38} + (-1) \cdot \frac{37}{38} = \frac{35 - 37}{38} = -\frac{2}{38} = -\frac{1}{19} \approx -0.0526 \). On average, you lose about 5.26 cents per $1 bet.

Variance \( \text{Var}(X) \): \( E(X^2) = 1225 \cdot \frac{1}{38} + 1 \cdot \frac{37}{38} = \frac{1262}{38} = \frac{631}{19} \approx 33.2105 \), \( [E(X)]^2 = \left(-\frac{1}{19}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{361} \approx 0.00277 \), \( \text{Var}(X) \approx 33.2077 \).

Standard Deviation \( \sigma(X) \): \( \sigma(X) = \sqrt{33.2077} \approx 5.7626 \). The net gain varies by about $5.76 from the mean.

Note: The negative \( E(X) \) reflects the house edge in roulette.

Example 5: NCAA Basketball Shot Expected Values
