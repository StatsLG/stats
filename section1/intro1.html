Theory

Basic Statistical Terminology

Statistics is the study of how to collect, organize, analyze, and interpret information...

  • Population: The entire collection of individuals or items of interest.
  • Sample: A subset of the population...
  • Data: The measurements collected from the sample.
  • Individuals: The objects described by the data.
  • Variable: A characteristic of an individual...
  • Statistic: A numerical characteristic of a sample.
  • Parameter: A fixed numerical characteristic of a population.
  • Census: Measurements taken from the entire population.

Branches of Statistics

Statistics is divided into two main branches:

  • Descriptive: Summarizes data without error.
  • Inferential: Uses a sample to infer about the population.

Types of Studies

  • Observational Study: Observes individuals without interfering.
  • Simulation: Uses models to replicate conditions.
  • Experiment: Applies treatments to measure responses.

Experiment Terminology: Subjects, treatment, treatment diagram.

Types of Data

  • Qualitative: Non-numerical (e.g., gender).
  • Quantitative: Numerical (discrete or continuous).

Levels of Measurement

  • Nominal: Categories with no order.
  • Ordinal: Ordered categories without true differences.
  • Interval: Ordered with differences but no true zero.
  • Ratio: Ordered with differences and true zero.
